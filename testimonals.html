Ms. Sai Chandana Divi spent her summer 2015 three-month internship within ASTUTE 2020 research group at Swansea University under my supervision. The ASTUTE 2020 (Advanced Sustainable Manufacturing Technologies) operation has been part-funded by the European Regional Development Fund through the Welsh Government and the participating Higher Education Institutions. The operation aims at embedding Advanced and Sustainable Technologies into High-Value Manufacturing across Wales by working collaboratively with Welsh businesses.

Ms. Divi had the opportunity to be part of a selected team from ASTUTE 2020 involved in collaborative research with the Aluminium Lighting Company (ALC). The team was responsible for studying the strength of lighting columns under various material characteristics and loading conditions. Moreover, ALC designed a novel hinge model for easy maintenance of the columns. It was necessary to study the performance of the proposed model design under different loading conditions.

Ms. Divi has taken part in various stages of the development of computational modelling of the hinge model. She was able to modify the design two models using Solid-works, study the mechanical behaviour of the system and finally analyse the performance under various loading conditions using ANSYS. As these models consists of a spring, it was necessary to characterise the spring, under the manufacturer standard, depending on the different loading conditions and performed buckling analysis of the spring to obtain the load multiplier.

Ms. Divi was able to work independently, paying attention to all the details in every aspect of the tasks leading to a high quality research work. At the end of her stay at Swansea, she submitted a very detailed research report and presentation, unveiling her depth knowledge in Computational Mechanics and research skills gained using both Solid-works and ANSYS.

With these skills, I believe she will be a perfect researcher and I strongly support her. I highly recommend her for the great research works that requires enthusiasm, intelligence, speed, patience and a positive attitude.

- Dr. Fawzi Belblidia, ASTUTE 2020 Senior Project Officer, Swansea Univerity, United Kingdom.
